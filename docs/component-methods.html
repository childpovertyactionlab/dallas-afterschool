Understanding Afterschool Programs in Dallas County

To understand the landscape of afterschool programs across Dallas County we assessed two components. The existing afterschool environment and current neighborhood characteristics. Comparing these components will help identify areas in need of additional afterschool programming.

Existing Afterschool Environment

Using program data from Dallas Afterschool, we developed a measure of the existing afterschool environment (supply) by comparing afterschool program density, and afterschool eligible children to total seats, and program count.

Current Neighborhood Conditions

We understand that the need for afterschool programs in any given neighborhood will vary based on the demographic characteristics of local families and children. To assess demand, we identified a number of characteristics for residents who may have a higher need for afterschool programming.

These characteristics include children experiencing food insecurity, , parents who travel longer distances to work or have irregular work schedules,, single parent households, and households facing unemployment. These are all considerations we took into account to assess current neighborhood conditions and demand for afterschool programming.
